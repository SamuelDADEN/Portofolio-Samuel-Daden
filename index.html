About

I am an engineering student specialising in aeronautics and mechanical systems. My academic training has led me to explore the intersection of classical mechanics, fluid dynamics, and modern numerical methods — from finite-element analysis to computational aerodynamics.

I am particularly interested in constrained optimisation problems: finding the lightest structure, the most efficient wing shape, or the optimal control-surface sizing — always with physical insight guiding the mathematics.

Feel free to reach out to discuss a project or an opportunity.

Reading

How to Build a Car

Adrian Newey · 2017

Currently reading Engineering · Memoir

Newey's memoir traces his career designing championship Formula 1 cars, from early aerodynamic experiments to the dominant Red Bull RB series. A rare insight into the engineering decisions — suspension geometry, aerodynamic packaging, tyre behaviour — that separate tenths of seconds on a circuit.

Never Split the Difference

Chris Voss · 2016

Read Negotiation · Psychology

A former FBI hostage negotiator shares field-tested techniques for high-stakes negotiation. The core argument — that emotional intelligence and tactical empathy outperform purely rational bargaining — translates naturally to engineering contexts: managing stakeholders, presenting design trade-offs, or defending a technical choice.
